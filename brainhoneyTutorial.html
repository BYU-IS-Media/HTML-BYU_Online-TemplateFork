LOG IN



Go to is.byu.edu and log in with your info. You will then be directed to a page similar to the one on the right. Click “Login.”

You will be taken to BYU’s CAS login area. Use your credentials and click “Sign In.”

Home Screen



The system will then take you to the Home Screen. Any classes you are an instructor for will appear here.
Click “View” to view the content of the course, “Gradebook” to see and add grades, and “Objectives” to see how students are doing learning the material.

Assignments ready to be graded will appear here.

The total number of students in all your classes, as well as the overall performance of your students, will appear here.

Any students who are “Critical” (meaning they failed the most recent assignments) will appear in a list here.

Click on “View” for a course to see a screen similar to the one above.

Gradebook



Click on “Gradebook” to see this screen.
You can click on any score to change it or click on a blank space in the table to add a score.

You can click on a score to bring up a screen like the one shown. Here you can modify the score or allow a retry, but make sure to click “Submit Score.”
*This one shows a quiz, allowing you to see what questions the student got right and wrong.

If you use a separate way to keep scores, you can input them into the Gradebook or export the scores to an Excel spreadsheet through clicking these buttons.

These tabs at the top allow you to view assignments based on their categories.

Click on “Objectives” to view the learning mastery of your class.

Click on “Test Analytics” to see this screen.

Click on a separate file to see the available quizzes in that section.
Then you can click on that quiz and see how well your students are doing for each quiz and question.

Click on “Final Grades” to submit grades at the end of the semester/term.

You can adjust scores by adding new calculations.

You will need to click “Report Final Grades” when everything is set to your satisfaction.

Tools



Click on “Tools” to send out emails to your students or make Announcements that your students will see on their end of BrainHoney.
