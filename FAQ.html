Frequently Asked Questions

Enrollment

    • How strict is enrollment or maximum classroom size?
    • Enrollment is currently capped at 30 students per section.
    • How do I enroll in the online class?
    • You may enroll for a BYU Online course the same way as you would any other course offered on campus. Using AIM, find the desired course, and then click “Add” next to the BYU Online section.
    • When can I apply for my online class?
    • BYU Online enrollment is under the same schedule as regular campus courses. You can enroll for the online course beginning on your priority registration date.
    • I just got added to the class and didn’t receive an email from anyone. Who can I talk to get additional information about my course?
    • Please contact your professor or BYU Online technical support with any questions.
    • What are the prerequisites for online courses?
    • BYU Online courses are offered to all students with Day Continuing enrollment status.
    • How much do online courses cost?
    • Tuition for online courses is included in the regular semester tuition paid by Day Continuing students.
    • Why is there a waiting list for some online courses?
    • Enrollment is currently capped at 30 students per section to ensure a favorable TA-student ratio.
    • Can classes be taken out of state?
    • Yes. You may even take courses out of the country—perfect while you’re on a study abroad.

General

    • What are the major differences between BYU Online and Independent Study?
    • BYU Online courses are different from Independent Study courses in three main ways: (1) online courses are included in your BYU tuition, while Independent Study courses come at an additional cost; (2) online courses follow a semester schedule, while Independent Study courses allow one year for completion; and (3) online courses have class size limits, while Independent Study has no enrollment caps.
    • How are online courses formatted?
    • BYU Online courses use a system called BrainHoney to provide instructional content, assignments, and quizzes. Each course is designed and verified by our in-house team of instructional designers to yield the most efficient and effective learning experience possible.
    • Is the teacher’s voice lost in the online format?
    • The assigned instructor writes each course. If you define “voice” as the instructor’s personality, then you can rest assured that the online format will favor your instructor’s beloved idiosyncrasies.
    • How long do I have to complete this course?
    • BYU Online courses must be completed by the end of the semester. Each course includes assignment deadlines designed to help you complete the course on time. You are free to work ahead, and can complete the course as early as the first day of the second block of the semester. (LINK to ACADEMIC CALENDAR)
    • What is a Checkpoint?
    • Checkpoints are scheduled meetings between you and your TA to review your progress in the class and answer any questions you may have.
    • What materials do I need to complete the course?
    • In addition to any required textbooks, you will need these items to complete the checkpoints:

      1. Webcam – Any webcam will work. We recommend using the “Logitec HD Webcam C270” (it’s available from Amazon.com).

      2. Microphone Headset – Any microphone headset will work. We recommend using the “Cyber Acoustics Universal Stereo Headset AC-204” (again, it’s available from Amazon.com).

Exams

    • Are tests given online or in the testing center?
    • Exams are proctored in an online testing format, designed to complement the nature of BYU Online courses .

Orientation

    • How do I know the location of my course’s orientation meeting?
    • Your professor or TA will email you the week before classes begin to provide details about your course’s orientation meeting time and location.
    • Can I attend the orientation meeting via the Internet?
    • Orientation meetings are the only aspects of the BYU Online courses that must be attended in person. Your TA may offer a recording of the orientation meeting after it ends, though a recording is never guaranteed.
    • Why is there a mandatory orientation meeting on campus for an online course?
    • We chose a face-to-face orientation meeting format to allow students to get to know their professor and TA personally before beginning work. Your instructor and TA will define course expectations, provide a general course overview, and answer any questions you may have.
